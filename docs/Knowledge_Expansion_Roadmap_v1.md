# Humanity Vault Knowledge Expansion Roadmap v1

## 1. Purpose

This document is the first official Knowledge Expansion Roadmap for
Humanity Vault AI. It reviews current knowledge coverage under
`knowledge/` and proposes a prioritized list of articles to author
next, organized into Top 10, Top 25, and Top 50 tiers. It is a
planning reference for sequencing future article-authoring work under
[Knowledge_Authoring_Standard_v1.md](Knowledge_Authoring_Standard_v1.md).

This document does not create, modify, or remove any articles or
application code. It is recommendations only.

## 2. Current Coverage

- 10 category folders exist under `knowledge/`, each with a
  placeholder `README.md`
- 3 articles currently exist (marked `[EXISTS]` throughout this
  roadmap):
  - Water Purification Using Boiling (Survival)
  - Basic CPR Overview (Medicine)
  - Daily Morning and Evening Dua (Islam)
- All other categories currently have no authored articles

## 3. Prioritization Rationale

Every recommendation in this roadmap is evaluated against four
criteria:

- **Survival value** - does it prevent death or serious harm in the
  near term?
- **Educational value** - does it teach a transferable skill or
  underlying principle?
- **Offline usefulness** - does it require no tools or internet
  access beyond what is realistically available post-disruption?
- **Civilization preservation value** - does it help a community
  rebuild or maintain organized society over years or generations?

Top 10 and Top 25 deliberately concentrate on Survival, Medicine, and
Agriculture - the categories with the most immediate life-or-death
impact. Construction, Electronics, and Engineering scale up sharply
in the Top 50, representing "rebuilding phase" knowledge (shelter,
power, tools). History, Philosophy, Islam, and Local Wisdom are
weighted toward civilization-preservation and educational value -
lower urgency, but essential for a community to remain organized,
ethical, and culturally continuous over time.

## 4. Top 10 Articles (Highest Overall Priority)

1. **[Survival] Water Purification Using Boiling** `[EXISTS]`
   Already covered. Clean water is the #1 survival priority - without
   it, nothing else matters within days.

2. **[Medicine] Basic CPR Overview** `[EXISTS]`
   Already covered. Reverses the most immediately fatal medical
   emergency with zero equipment.

3. **[Medicine] Wound Care & Bleeding Control** `[EXISTS]`
   Severe bleeding kills faster than almost anything else; basic
   pressure/dressing techniques save lives with minimal supplies.

4. **[Survival] Fire Starting Without Matches**
   Fire enables warmth, cooking, water purification (boiling), and
   morale - foundational to nearly every other survival task.

5. **[Agriculture] Growing Staple Food Crops** (grains, tubers,
   legumes)
   Food security underpins long-term survival; staple crops are
   calorie-dense and storable, the backbone of any community.

6. **[Survival] Emergency Shelter Construction** `[EXISTS]`
   Exposure (cold/heat/rain) can kill within hours; shelter-building
   with available materials is core survival knowledge.
   (Implemented as "Basic Shelter Construction".)

7. **[Agriculture] Seed Saving & Storage**
   Without seed banks, one bad harvest ends food production
   permanently - this is civilization-preservation knowledge.

8. **[Survival] Food & Water Storage and Preservation** `[EXISTS]`
   Bridges the gap between harvests/resupply; prevents waste and
   famine during shortages.
   (Food preservation portion implemented as "Food Preservation
   Basics"; water storage guidance remains a future addition.)

9. **[Medicine] Sanitation, Hygiene & Disease Prevention**
   Historically, poor sanitation has killed more people than war or
   famine combined - simple hygiene practices prevent epidemics.

10. **[Survival] Basic Navigation Without GPS or Compass**
    Getting lost or unable to relocate resources/people is a major
    risk when modern infrastructure (GPS, maps, networks) is gone.

## 6. Top 50 Articles (Top 25 + 25 Additional, by Category)

### Survival (+3 new, 5 total)

- **Trapping & Hunting Basics**
  Supplemental protein source when farming/livestock is not yet
  established.
- **Basic Knot Tying for Survival Tasks**
  Cheap, universal skill underlying shelter, tools, traps, and rescue.
- **Signaling for Rescue** (visual/audio signals)
  Increases chance of being found by search/rescue or other survivors.

### Medicine (+3 new, 6 total)

- **Childbirth Basics & Newborn Care Without a Hospital**
  Birth will continue regardless of infrastructure; basic safe
  practices reduce maternal/infant mortality drastically.
- **Treating Burns** (assessment & first aid)
  Common injury around fire/cooking; improper treatment causes severe
  complications.
- **Managing Chronic Conditions Without Pharmacy Access** (e.g.
  diabetes, hypertension - dietary/lifestyle management)
  Many people depend on ongoing medication; basic management knowledge
  reduces sudden health crises.

### Agriculture (+3 new, 5 total)

- **Composting & Soil Fertility Management**
  Sustains crop yields long-term without synthetic fertilizer supply
  chains.
- **Animal Husbandry Basics** (poultry, goats, etc.)
  Provides protein, labor, and renewable resources (eggs, milk, manure)
  with modest space/skill requirements.
- **Natural Pest & Disease Control for Crops**
  Crop loss to pests can be as devastating as a failed harvest;
  low-tech controls are essential without pesticides.

### Construction (+3 new, 6 total)

- **Sanitation Systems** (latrines, waste management)
  Prevents groundwater contamination and disease - directly
  complements Medicine's hygiene article.
- **Basic Carpentry Joints & Techniques**
  Enables durable furniture, tool handles, and structural repairs
  without nails/hardware.
- **Roofing & Waterproofing Methods**
  Protects shelters and stored food/seed from rain damage and mold.

### Electronics (+2 new, 4 total)

- **Solar Power Basics** (panels, charge controllers, batteries)
  Renewable electricity for lighting, communication, and medical
  equipment - high long-term value if panels are salvaged/available.
- **Battery Repair, Charging & Reuse**
  Extends the usable life of the most common portable power source.

### Engineering (+2 new, 5 total)

- **Basic Blacksmithing & Metalworking**
  Enables tool repair and creation (knives, hinges, nails) - a
  cornerstone trade for any self-sufficient community.
- **Water Pumps & Irrigation Systems**
  Moves water efficiently for agriculture and sanitation, multiplying
  the value of a single water source.

### History (+2 new, 4 total)

- **Timeline of Major Inventions & Underlying Principles**
  Helps communities "rediscover" technology faster by understanding
  why/how past inventions worked.
- **Preserving Records & Archiving Methods** (durable media, copying
  practices)
  Ensures Humanity Vault's own knowledge (and community records)
  survive physical degradation and time.

### Philosophy (+2 new, 4 total)

- **Community Governance & Conflict Resolution**
  Small groups need fair, agreed-upon ways to make decisions and
  resolve disputes without formal courts.
- **Foundations of Education for the Next Generation**
  Knowledge preservation is meaningless without a way to teach it
  forward; covers basic teaching methods with minimal materials.

### Islam (+2 new, 4 total)

- **Islamic Guidance on Community, Charity & Mutual Aid in Hardship**
  Provides a values framework for resource-sharing and care for the
  vulnerable during crises.
- **Contributions of Islamic Civilization to Science & Knowledge**
  Preserves historical context showing how scientific knowledge and
  faith have coexisted and advanced together.

### Local Wisdom (+3 new, 4 total)

- **Traditional Herbal Remedies & Natural Medicine**
  Complements formal medicine where pharmaceuticals are unavailable;
  captures knowledge at risk of being lost.
- **Traditional Food Fermentation & Preservation Techniques**
  Low-tech, low-energy methods (fermenting, smoking, salting) extend
  food shelf life significantly.
- **Oral History Preservation Techniques**
  Captures community-specific survival knowledge, genealogy, and
  history that isn't written down anywhere else.

  survival-value criterion.

  Top 25 and Top 50 - these represent "rebuilding phase" knowledge

  needs are met.

  essential for a community to remain organized, ethical, and
  culturally continuous over years.
