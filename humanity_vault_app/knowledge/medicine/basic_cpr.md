# Basic CPR Overview

## Summary

Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) is an emergency procedure used to manually preserve brain function until further measures restore spontaneous blood circulation and breathing in a person who is in cardiac arrest.

## Main Content

To perform basic CPR, place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, place the other hand on top, and push hard and fast at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute, allowing the chest to fully recoil between compressions. Continue until emergency help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

## Benefits

- Can double or triple a cardiac arrest victim's chance of survival
- Helps maintain blood flow to vital organs until help arrives
- Can be performed without specialized equipment
