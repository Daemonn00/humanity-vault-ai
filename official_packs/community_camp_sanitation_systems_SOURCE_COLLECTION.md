### Source: CDC (archived), Potential Sanitation Solutions During an Emergency Response

- **Citation/URL:** https://archive.cdc.gov/www_cdc_gov/healthywater/global/sanitation/sanitation-emergency-response.html
- **Publisher:** Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
- **Which claims it supports:** A2, A3, B1, B2
- **Source quality tier:** [x] Tier 1 (official U.S. federal public-
  health agency; already used successfully as a source in this
  project's household water article)
- **Directly opened and read in full:** Yes - six immediate-term
  sanitation technologies (packet latrines, bucket/elevated toilets,
  chemical toilets, trench latrines, pit latrines, eco-san latrines),
  a phased strategy (immediate phase using chemical toilets or
  trenches/pits; targeted deployment of elevated latrines for high
  water tables), Sphere-standard figures ("50 people per latrine"
  initially, improving "toward 20 people per latrine"), and the
  principle "install handwashing stations at every sanitation
  facility, regardless of the type."
- **Limitations:** An archived CDC page (archive.cdc.gov) - current
  live cdc.gov may have superseded content; the archived version was
  the only one found and directly readable this pass.
- **Collected by:** Claude (AI agent)
- **Date:** 2026-07-19

### Source: SSWM.info, Immediate and Short-Term Emergency Sanitation

- **Citation/URL:** https://sswm.info/water-nutrient-cycle/wastewater-treatment/hardwares/sanitation-emergencies/immediate-and-short-term-emergency-sanitation

  reference, directly opened and read)
- **Directly opened and read in full:** Yes - the "wrap, contain, and
  collect" strategy for scattered/in-situ populations; trench latrine
  dimensions ("20 to 30cm wide and 15cm deep" for shallow trenches, up

  filled up completely (20cm beneath the slab)"); alternative
  technologies (borehole latrines, Peepoo bags, urine-diverting

### Source: Oxfam WASH, Domestic and Refugee Camp Waste Management

- **Citation/URL:** https://www.oxfamwash.org/domestic-and-refugee-camp-waste-management/
- **Publisher:** Oxfam
- **Which claims it supports:** D1, D2, D3, D4, D5 (partial), D6, D7
- **Source quality tier:** [x] Tier 1/2
- **Directly opened and read in full:** Yes - household-to-collection-
  point distances ("no more than 100 meters," tightening to "10
  meters" in initial response); communal bin ratio ("one 100-litre bin
  should serve approximately 10 families," "at least 15 meters from
  residences"); transport scaling (hand-carts "10,000-20,000 people
  within 1km," trucks for populations "exceeding 50,000"); burial-pit
  siting ("at least 30m away from the nearest dwelling" and water
  sources, "minimum 1.5 meters above the water table," daily soil
  cover); the brief mention "no medical waste should be disposed of in
  these pits" (D5 - flagged as requiring deeper sourcing, see Research
  Plan); incineration cautions (plastics removed first, downwind,
  informal burning often fails to reach adequate temperature);
  reference ratios (6 m³ per 50 people/week for burial, 3 m³ per 200
  people/week for landfill design).
- **Collected by:** Claude (AI agent)
- **Date:** 2026-07-19

### Source: Global WASH Cluster eCompendium, Single Ventilated Improved Pit (S.4)

- **Citation/URL:** https://www.emersan-compendium.org/en/technologies/technology/single-ventilated-improved-pit-vip
