### Claim D1

**Article:** 4 - Thermal Management, Ventilation, and Fire Safety in
Shelter Systems

**Why safety-critical:** Carbon monoxide poisoning from indoor fuel-
burning devices is a leading cause of accidental death in exactly the
sheltering-in-place/temporary-heating scenario this article addresses.
A wrong or softened claim here could directly cause death.

**Proposed drafting boundary:** Fuel-burning devices - gas stoves and
ovens, barbeque/charcoal grills, gasoline generators, and any vehicle
- must never be operated indoors, in an attached garage, or in any
poorly ventilated/enclosed space, because of carbon monoxide
poisoning risk. This is a categorical rule, not a conditional one.

**Source:** U.S. Fire Administration (USFA/FEMA), "Carbon Monoxide"
page, and U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), "Power Outages
and Indoor Air Quality" page - two independent Tier 1 federal sources.

**Source access location:**
https://www.usfa.fema.gov/prevention/life-safety-hazards/carbon-monoxide/
and
https://www.epa.gov/emergencies-iaq/power-outages-and-indoor-air-quality-iaq

**What the source actually says:**
> USFA: "CO poisoning can occur when a fuel-burning appliance or
> machine, such as a furnace, heater or generator, is not working or
> vented properly."

> EPA: "do not try to heat your home by using combustion appliances
> including gas stoves or ovens, barbeque grills or dryers." "Never
> operate any gas-burning heater or other appliance in a poorly
> vented or closed room." "Do not run a car or truck inside a garage
> attached to your house, even if you leave the door open."

**What the Founder should verify:** that the proposed drafting
boundary's categorical "never" framing matches both sources' own
categorical framing, and does not soften into a conditional that
either source does not itself support.

### Claim D2

**Article:** 4

**Why safety-critical:** Generator placement is the specific,
actionable mitigation for D1's hazard - a wrong distance/location
claim directly enables CO poisoning.

**Proposed drafting boundary:** Portable generators must be run
outdoors, far from the building, and specifically away from doors,
windows, vents, and any area where people are sleeping - never inside
a house, garage, or on a balcony.

**Source:** Same two sources as D1.

**Source access location:** Same two URLs as D1.

**What the source actually says:**
> USFA: "Use portable generators outdoors in well-ventilated areas
> away from all doors, windows and vents."

> EPA: "Do use portable generators outside and far away from
> buildings." "Do not use portable generators under any of the
> following conditions: inside your house or garage; on balconies or
> near doors, vents or windows, and; near where you or your children
> are sleeping."

**What the Founder should verify:** that the proposed boundary
combines both sources accurately without adding a distance figure
(e.g. a specific number of feet/meters) that neither source states -
both sources give qualitative placement rules only, no numeric
distance.

### Claim D3

**Article:** 4

**Why safety-critical:** CO alarms are the last line of detection if
D1/D2 are somehow violated or a device fails - incorrect placement
guidance could leave a sleeping area undetected.

**Proposed drafting boundary:** Install carbon monoxide alarms in a
central location outside each separate sleeping area, and on every
level of the shelter/dwelling.

### Claim D4

**Article:** 4

**Why safety-critical:** Vented fireplaces/stoves are a legitimate,
recommended heating option (per D5's contrast and general shelter
guidance) - but only if used correctly; each of these precautions
individually prevents a real CO or fire hazard.

**Proposed drafting boundary:** If using a vented fireplace or wood
stove for heat: have flues and chimneys inspected before each heating
season for leakage/blockage, open the damper before lighting and keep
it open until ashes are fully cool, never close the damper or leave
the space with warm ashes still present, never burn charcoal in a
fireplace (CO risk), and burn only dry, seasoned wood.

**Source:** EPA, "Power Outages and Indoor Air Quality" page.

**Source access location:**
https://www.epa.gov/emergencies-iaq/power-outages-and-indoor-air-quality-iaq

**What the source actually says:**
> "Have flues and chimneys inspected before each heating season for
> leakage and blockage by creosote or debris." "Open the fireplace
> damper before lighting the fire and keep it open until the ashes
> are cool." "Never close the damper or go to bed if the ashes are
> still warm." "Never use charcoal in a fireplace because there is a
> risk of carbon monoxide poisoning." "Burn only dry, seasoned wood
> and maintain a hot fire."

**What the Founder should verify:** that all five precautions are
included together (per the source's own "if using a fireplace, do all
of these" framing) - dropping any one of the five while presenting the
rest as complete guidance would misrepresent the source.

### Claim D6

**Article:** 4

**Why safety-critical:** Space heaters are named by USFA's own data as
disproportionately dangerous (3% of home heating fires, 41% of fatal
heating fires) - exactly the equipment likely to be used in an
emergency/transitional shelter without central heating.

**Proposed drafting boundary:** Keep anything that can burn at least 3
feet from any heat source (space heaters, fireplaces, wood stoves,
radiators, candles); turn space heaters off when leaving the room or
going to bed; never use an oven to heat a shelter.

**Source:** USFA, "Heating Fire Safety" page - a different USFA page
from D1-D3's Carbon Monoxide page.

**Source access location:**
https://www.usfa.fema.gov/prevention/home-fires/prevent-fires/heating/

**What the source actually says:**
> "Keep anything that can burn at least 3 feet from all heat sources
> including fireplaces, wood stoves, radiators, space heaters or
> candles." "Turn space heaters off when leaving the room or going to
> bed." "Never use an oven to heat your home."

**What the Founder should verify:** that the 3-foot figure is
preserved exactly (not rounded, not converted to metric without
also keeping the original figure) and not implied to be a universal
clearance for every heater type - the source states one general
figure across several heat-source types, not a per-device engineering
specification.

**Founder Decision:**

[ ] CONFIRMED
[ ] REJECTED
