This pack preserves practical knowledge for staying findable and
oriented when normal infrastructure and equipment aren't available:
recognizing that an official visual signal system exists for
communicating with search aircraft, general radio operating etiquette
and battery care, an improvised backup compass technique, and basic
knots and lashing for shelter framing and securing gear. Each article
is written for a general reader with no assumed equipment, training,
or prior experience, and stays within general, well-established
practical knowledge rather than reproducing any official code, table,
or exact technical specification - readers who need the complete,
official version of the visual signal code are directed to the FAA's
own publication.
