# Burn Assessment and Field Treatment

## Summary

Cooling a burn promptly with clean, cool running water - and avoiding several common but harmful home remedies - are widely recognized first steps for a thermal burn. This article covers general burn first aid; electrical and chemical burns need different handling, and any burn beyond a small, minor one needs professional medical care.

## Main Content

For an ordinary thermal burn (from heat, not electricity or chemicals), do not start first aid before making sure you are safe first (for example, switching off an electrical current or wearing gloves around a chemical). Cool the burned area with clean, running water. Avoid using ice directly on a burn, since ice can deepen the injury, and avoid prolonged cooling, since cooling for too long can lead to hypothermia - the goal is to cool the burn, not to create a new problem.

Several traditional home remedies should not be used on a burn: do not apply paste, oil, or turmeric (haldi), and do not apply raw cotton directly to the burn. Do not apply any material directly to the wound, since it might become infected, and do not apply topical medication until the person has been placed under appropriate medical care. Once cooled, wrap the person in a clean cloth or sheet and arrange transport to the nearest appropriate medical care.

Do not open blisters until topical antimicrobials can be applied by a health-care provider. An intact blister helps protect the healing skin underneath.

Electrical burns and chemical burns need different first steps than an ordinary thermal burn. For an electrical burn, make sure the power source is actually off before touching the person - a live electrical source can injure a rescuer too. For a chemical burn, remove or dilute the chemical by irrigating the area with large amounts of water - but exact guidance can vary by the specific chemical involved, so this article does not attempt to cover chemical-specific handling.

Any burn that is large, deep, on the face, hands, or genitals, caused by electricity or chemicals, or that shows signs of infection needs professional medical care - field first aid is meant to limit damage while that care is arranged, not to replace it.

## Benefits

- Cooling promptly can reduce how deep and severe a burn becomes
- Avoiding harmful home remedies prevents making the injury worse
- Recognizing when electrical or chemical burns need different handling can prevent a rescuer from being injured too
