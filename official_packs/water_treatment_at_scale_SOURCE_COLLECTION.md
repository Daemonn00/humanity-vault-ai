### Source: Global WASH Cluster / German WASH Network / FHNW / SuSanA eCompendium ("emergency-wash.org")

- **Citation/URL:** https://www.emergency-wash.org/water/en/technologies/ (multiple technology pages, listed individually below)
- **Publisher, confirmed by direct read of its own credits page:** a
  collaborative effort of Global WASH Cluster partners (a UN-
  coordinated inter-agency humanitarian cluster), the German WASH
  Network, University of Applied Science and Arts Northwestern
  Switzerland (FHNW), and SuSanA, with international sector-expert
  contributions.
- **Which claims it supports:** Article 1 (Claims 1, 2), Article 2
  (all claims), Article 3 (Claims 1, 2, 3, 5), Article 4 (Claims 1-4,
  6, and 5), Article 5 (all claims).
- **Source quality tier:** [x] Tier 1 (Global WASH Cluster is a UN-
  coordinated official inter-agency body; this is its own published
  technical compendium, not a secondhand description of it)
- **Directly opened and read in full, per page:**
  - [x] T.6 Chlorination - dosing 1-6 mg/L (0.5-2 mg/L non-turbid),
    30-min contact standard, FRC 0.2-0.5 mg/L target, turbidity <5
    NTU requirement, pH<8, jar-test method, trihalomethane by-product
    caution.
  - [x] H.7 Point-of-Supply Chlorination - tablet/venturi dosing
    mechanisms, ~2 mg/L target, 10-30 L/min flow, 1,500-2,500 L per
    tablet, turbidity <5 NTU precondition.
  - [x] T.4 (Assisted) Sedimentation - jar-test method, 1-hour settling
    rule of thumb, 80% clarity criterion, turbidity target <5 NTU,
    aluminum coagulant pH 6-8, aluminum health limit 0.2 mg/L.
  - [x] T.1 Roughing Filtration - up to 500 NTU influent, ~90%
    turbidity reduction, ~10 NTU effluent (paired with slow sand
    filtration), 4-25 mm graded media, 0.3-1.5 m/h flow, 60-99%
    bacterial reduction (pre-treatment only, not a substitute for
    disinfection).
  - [x] T.9 Slow Sand Filtration - turbidity <10 NTU (peaks to 50
    tolerated), flow 0.1-0.4 m/h, sand depth 0.6-1.2 m, water height
    1.0-1.5 m, >95% bacteria/virus removal, >99% Giardia/Cryptosporidium
    removal, effluent <1 NTU, maintenance by scraping top sand layer.
  - [x] D.7 Community Distribution System - ≥5 m residual pressure,
    ≥0.125 L/s per tap, ≤250 users per tap, ≥15 L/person/day, ≤500 m
    walking distance, ≤30-minute round trip, pipe velocity 0.7-3 m/s,
    cross-contamination risks (negative pressure at leaky joints,
    water pipes above wastewater pipes).
  - [x] D.6 Water Storage Tank (Long-Term, Locally Built) - sizing to
    daily demand plus contingency (≥1 day, up to 3 days for solar/wind
    pumping), construction materials, ventilation/screening
    requirements, shock chlorination at 50 mg/L (explicitly a tank-
    cleaning concentration, not routine drinking-water dosing).
  - [x] Safe Water Storage (H.1) - container material/design
    requirements, 7.5-15 L/person/day, two 10-20 L containers per
    household recommendation.
  - [x] "/water/en" credits page - publisher identification (above).
- **Collected by:** Claude (AI agent)
- **Date:** 2026-07-17

### Source: Oxfam WASH ("oxfamwash.org")

- **Citation/URL:**

- **Which claims it supports:** Article 4 (Claims 1-4, 6 -

- **Source quality tier:** [x] Tier 1 (Oxfam is an established
  international humanitarian organization; this is its own published
  WASH technical guidance)
- **Directly opened and read in full:**
  - [x] "Chlorination in Emergencies" - dosing 2.0 mg/L (<10 NTU) /
    4.0-5.0 mg/L (10-100 NTU), 30-min contact at 25°C (doubling per
    10°C drop), FRC 0.2-0.5 mg/L at point of delivery (attributed to
    Sphere), 0.2 mg/L minimum at household consumption, upper limit
    4.0-5.0 mg/L (never exceed), palatability concerns above 1.0-2.0
    mg/L, DPD1 test method (0.2-5 mg/L range), test strips (0-5 mg/L),
    daily operational monitoring.
  - [x] "Water Quality Analysis in Emergency Situations" - testing
    parameters by emergency phase, monitoring frequency by source type
    (open wells monthly, covered/piped twice yearly, rainwater
    yearly), E. coli/100mL classification (0 compliant, 1-10

    chemical guideline values (arsenic 0.01 mg/L, fluoride 1.5 mg/L,
    nitrate 11 mg/L as N, manganese 0.4 mg/L health-based / 0.1 mg/L
    taste-rejection, iron 0.3 mg/L taste threshold).
